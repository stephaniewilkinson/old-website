About

Hi! I'm Stephanie. I'm a web developer with four years of experience with Ruby and frameworks like Roda and Rails, and have leveled up my Javascript skills at General Assembly for the MEAN stack. I am the Technology Director at the California Association of Independent Schools.

I live in LA and have been working since 2009 with schools and the nonprofit sector, and I love to give back to my community by teaching RailsGirls and other volunteering. I'm active in the LA programmer community and run a weekly programming meetup called Ruby in Hollywood. I also have a lot of side projects in various languages and frameworks. Right now I'm working on a Phoenix app using Phoenix channels.

I built and manage a Rails app for the California Association of Independent Schools, and travel the state giving talks and workshops to teach schools how to use the app.

Languages: JavaScript, Ruby, HTML, CSS, Python

Libraries/Frameworks: jQuery, Node.js, Mongoose, Express.js, AngularJS, Rails, Roda

Databases: Mongo & PostgreSQL

Related Skills: Agile, Git, Project Management, Pivotal Tracker, Vim, Teaching

Ruby in Hollywood is a weekly hack night for programmers of all stripes. We don't always do Ruby, and we don't always meet in Hollywood, but we always share and learn from each other.

RailsGirls
Volunteer

Our aim is to give tools and a community for women to understand technology and to build their ideas. We do this by providing a great experience on building things and by making technology more approachable.

Learn sketching, prototyping, basic programming and get introduced to the world of technology. Rails Girls was born in Finland, but is nowadays a global, non-profit volunteer community.
